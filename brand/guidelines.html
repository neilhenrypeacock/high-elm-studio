01 — Logo

the mark and
the word.

The elm leaf is the brand mark — an organic form representing High Elm Farm, Norfolk, the origin of the name. It is always accompanied by the veining detail. The wordmark "high elm studio." is always lowercase with a trailing period.

Primary — light background
Mark sizes
footer
nav
section
cta
full
Reversed — dark background
Stacked format

logo rules

Do
  • Use primary (teal) mark on sand, stone, or white backgrounds
  • Use reversed (sand) mark on night or ink backgrounds
  • Keep the trailing period in "high elm studio."
  • Maintain veining lines — they are part of the mark
  • Preserve the leaf's aspect ratio (60:100)
Don't
  • Recolour the leaf in non-brand colours
  • Write "High Elm Studio" with capital letters
  • Omit the trailing period from the wordmark
  • Add drop shadows, gradients, or effects
  • Stretch, skew, or distort the leaf form
02 — Colour

warm, grounded,
with one calm accent.

The palette is built around earthy neutrals — sand, stone, mid — with a single teal accent (ocean) used for emphasis. Never use pure black or pure white.

03 — Typography

two fonts,
two distinct roles.

League Spartan · 600 · Display / Headlines
Grounded thinking
for an unsteady moment.

Use for: hero headlines, section titles, editorial pull quotes. Weight 600 with tight letter-spacing (-0.05em). Always lowercase.

Raleway · Weight 200 · Body / Paragraphs
The businesses who communicate clearly, automate intelligently, and act decisively are the ones who pull ahead. AI is just the latest — and most powerful — tool to make that possible.

Use for: all body copy, paragraphs, longer explanatory text. Weight 200–300. Line height 1.85–1.95.

League Spartan · Weight 600 · UI / Interface

Use for: navigation, buttons, labels, captions, all interactive UI. Always lowercase. Tight letter spacing (-0.01em to -0.05em). Weights 300/400/600.

05 — Spacing

room to

The brand uses generous whitespace. Section padding starts at 80px and scales to 100px on desktop. Grid gaps are kept tight (2px) or generous (60–80px) — never in between.

06 — Tone of Voice

measured, expert,
never corporate.

The brand sounds like someone who has done the work — not someone selling the work. Plain over clever. Concrete over abstract. Human over corporate.

Don't write

"We leverage cutting-edge AI solutions to transform your enterprise workflow and deliver synergistic outcomes."

Write this instead

"We find where AI creates genuine leverage in your business — and build it so your team can actually use it."

never use

"cutting-edge" · "bleeding-edge" · "revolutionary" · "game-changing"
"solutions" · "synergy" · "ecosystem" · "seamless" · "robust"
"leverage" (as a verb) · exclamation marks

key phrases

"Grounded thinking for an unsteady moment." — tagline
"The first conversation is free — no obligation." — engagement model
"The gap is growing. Let's close it." — closing
"AI for business solutions. Not AI for AI's sake." — positioning
